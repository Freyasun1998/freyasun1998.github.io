1. Introduction

Temperature is a fundamental measurement for describing the climate, and the temperature in particular places can have wide-ranging effects on human life and ecosystems. For example, increases in air temperature can lead to more intense heat waves, which can cause illness and death, especially in vulnerable populations. Annual and seasonal temperature patterns also determine the types of animals and plants that can survive in particular locations. Changes in temperature can disrupt a wide range of natural processes, particularly if these changes occur more quickly than plant and animal species can adapt. Concentrations of heat-trapping greenhouse gases are increasing in the Earth's atmosphere. In response, average temperatures at the Earth's surface are increasing and are expected to continue rising. Because climate change can shift the wind patterns and ocean currents that drive the world's climate system, some areas are warming more than others, and some have experienced cooling.

However, regarding the existence of climate change, some say climate change is the biggest threat of our age while others say it's a myth based on dodgy science. We are turning some of the data over to you so you can form your own view.

One of the impacts of climate change that cannot be ignored is the phenomenon of drought. Drought is an event of prolonged shortages in the water supply, whether atmospheric (below-average precipitation), surface water or ground water. Throughout history, droughts are viewed as "disasters". Indicators of drought include precipitation, temperature, streamflow, ground and reservoir water levels, soil moisture, and snowpack. Temperature change increases the odds of worsening drought in many parts of the United States and the world. Severe drought can affect water supply, agriculture, transportation, energy, public health. These impacts can inflict extreme costs on people and business. In this project we are going to analyze the relationship between temperature change and drought phenomenon.

2. Method

2.1 Data Collection & Data Cleaning

2.1.1 Temperature Anomalies Worldwide

To get a general idea of global temperature change, we chose to use data offered by EPA, where information on temperature change from 1901 to the present is provided: Temperatures Worldwide. There are 4 columns in this dataset:

  • Year
  • Earth's surface: this indicator came from weather stations on land, and observations from buoys and ships on the ocean, thereby providing data from sites spanning much of the surface of the Earth. This indicator shows annual anomalies, or differences, compared with the average temperature from 1901 to 2000. For example, an anomaly of +2.0 degrees means the average temperature was 2 degrees higher than the long-term average. Anomalies have been calculated for each weather station. Daily temperature measurements at each site were used to calculate monthly anomalies, which were then averaged to find an annual temperature anomaly for each year. Global anomalies have been determined by dividing the world into a grid, averaging the data for each cell of the grid, and then averaging the grid cells together.
  • Lower troposphere (measured by satellite) (UAH) & Lower troposphere (measured by satellite) (RSS): measure the lower troposphere temperature of the Earth, which is the lowest level of the Earth's atmosphere, by satellite. "UAH" and "RSS" represent two different methods of analyzing the original satellite measurements.

There are several paragraphs within the dataset, so before jumping into data analysis part, we removed all the irrelevant and redundant information. Also, due to some technical restrictions, years before 1979 are marked as NAs, so we decide to drop all the missing values.

2.2 Design choices

2.2.1 Barplots for the Earth's Surface Temperature Anomalies

As mentioned, the temperature anomalies datesets contain time series data from 1901 to 2020, and since this indicator/this column represents temperature differences, compared with the average temperature from 1901 to 2000, therefore, we decided to use a bar chart and color-coded the years above the 220-year average and the years below the 220-year average. Specifically, the years above the average represent a year with a higher temperature comparatively and are marked in red, while the years below the average represent a year with a lower temperature compared to the overall average and are marked in blue. Thus, we can make a better visual comparison.

2.2.2 Stacked Area Charts for Temperature Anomalies Measured by Satellite

An area chart combines the line chart and bar chart to show how one or more groups' numeric values change over the progression of a second variable, typically that of time. An area chart is distinguished from a line chart by the addition of shading between lines and a baseline, like in a bar chart.

In this case, we decide to use stacked area chart. In the overlapping area chart, each line was shaded from its vertical value to a common baseline. In the stacked area chart, lines are plotted one at a time, with the height of the most recently-plotted group serving as a moving baseline. As such, the fully-stacked height of the topmost line will correspond to the total when summing across all groups.

In our case, when looking into the satellite measurements, there are different methods: "UAH" and "RSS". Since we do not have a clear perception of the advantages and disadvantages of these two measures, both of them are going to be used as references for our problem analysis. We intended to take the average value for them, but considering the fact that the difference between these two indicators are quite small so if we take the mean value for them, points or lines maybe overlap between each other. In view of the above reasons, we decided to use two area charts to representing satellite measurements for the whole world and the United States.

4. Discussion

4.1 Global Warming and Temperature Change in US

Recall that in the previous Fig.1, since 1977, the temperature anomaly has been above 0, meaning that it has been above the overall average for each of these years, and at the same time, the temperature anomaly is basically increasing, so we have global evidence of warming, and it is getting worse!

And to refine this issue, comparing the values of temperature changes in recent years for different countries and cities in different regions of the world, we find that the countries and cities with the strongest temperature changes, i.e. the largest temperature differences, seem to be mainly clustered in the Asian region (see Fig.5).

Based on the results of Fig.14, most states are experiencing warming, especially in winter. Actually, it can lead to one of the negative effects of global warming. Overwintering crops will start to grow because of false signals of early warming, but if there is a sudden drop in temperature, those crops will freeze altogether. And regional differences between warmer states could be due to differences in the amount of fossil fuels burned, which is one of the main causes of global warming. Therefore, advocating environmental protection and low-carbon economy, taking actions to deal with climate change, and promoting policies that will protect those most vulnerable to climate change impacts are important cornerstones of everyone's climate work.

4.2 Drought Phenomenon in US

The drought phenomenon in US especially in the western is much severe and may keep a long time. Just in the past few weeks, 45.01% of the U.S. and 53.77% of the lower 48 states are in drought.

From the Fig.13, we can find that sometimes the drought may happen after the temperature rises. It make sense that just the cumulative impact of temperature changes will lead to drought. For example, sometimes the low temperature in the last year may lead to a low annual degree of drought. In some areas, droughts can persist through a vicious cycle, in which very dry soils and diminished plant cover absorb more solar radiation and heat up, encouraging the formation of high pressure systems that further suppress rainfall, leading an already dry area to become even drier (Reference). So because of the continuous global warming, its impact especially the drought happen more frequently and more severe. The damage of drought has already affect some people's life and cause more serious consequence in US. For example, the agriculture of US suffered by drought a lot and it eventually has an impact to our economy. Regions such as the U.S. Southwest, where droughts are expected to get more frequent, intense, and longer lasting, are at particular risk. For northern part, the temperature increase would lead to many environmental problems including the raise of sea level. The thawed ice may make many animals lose their habitat. For southern part, the temperature increase the level and area of drought places The drought phenomenon would put more area under the threat of desertification.

5. Conclusion

Warmer temperatures enhance evaporation, which reduces surface water and dries out soils and vegetation. This makes periods with low precipitation drier than they would be in cooler conditions. These visualizations show that warming increases precipitation variability. Extreme precipitation always goes with drought. More periods of drought increase the amount of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere, which would reduce the amount of vegetation storing carbon dioxide and increase the average temperature in return. So we can conclude that temperature change and drought phenomenon are cause and result to each other.
